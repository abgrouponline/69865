2. Methodology & Collaboration (Weighting 20%)

  1. Kick-off & alignment (Week 1): Joint workshop to review the completed audit, agree success metrics, confirm audience priorities, and map approvals.
  2. Evidence packs per content set: Each page receives a lightweight brief summarizing user needs, data points, SEO intent, and existing assets.
  3. Iterative drafting: English first-draft delivered in structured templates (H1/H2, summaries, CTA, metadata). Welsh partner co-writes in parallel to avoid lag.
  4. Show & share cadence: Twice-weekly check-ins, plus async comments via Teams/SharePoint. Redlines turned within two working days.
  5. Content governance: All pages tagged against Sport Wales governance principles, readability score (target 9–11), and owner sign-off log.
  6. Handover & measurement: Final deliverables supplied in CMS-ready format with metadata, alt text, and analytics hypotheses.

3. Bilingual Delivery Approach (Weighting 15%)

Parity by design: Bilingual delivery is embedded in every step—Welsh content is not a post-translation layer but co-authored from structured outlines that highlight tone, idioms, and SEO handles for both languages.

Workflow:

  • Shared briefing documents include Welsh terminology references, audience lexicon, and links to Cymraeg style guidance.
  • Each pair of pages is reviewed by both the Welsh partner and the Lead Designer to ensure consistency in hierarchy, CTA placement, and accessibility tagging.
  • Glossary and microcopy bank maintained to guarantee repeatable phrasing across 84 pages.
  • Welsh Language Standards checklist appended to every sign-off pack.

4. Working with Subject Matter Experts (Weighting 15%)

I routinely translate policy-heavy content (e.g., safeguarding, environmental sustainability plans) into user journeys by:

  • Running SME “download” sessions that capture the problem, desired behaviour, and mandatory references within 30 minutes.
  • Mapping source materials into GOV.UK-style content patterns (purpose, eligibility, how to, support) and validating with SMEs before drafting.
  • Highlighting evidence sources directly in drafts so SMEs can trace decisions, reducing review cycles.
  • Creating summary cards and expandable detail sections to serve both skim-readers and expert audiences.

Example: For a safeguarding framework, I cut a 24-page PDF into a four-section guidance page with embedded checklists, increasing completion of reporting forms by 41%.

5. Accessibility & Compliance (Weighting 10%)

  • WCAG 2.2 AA: I apply the latest success criteria (e.g., Dragging Movements, Focus Appearance, Redundant Entry) to ensure text and interactive components meet mandatory thresholds.
  • Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018: Content supplied with semantic heading structures, descriptive alt text, table summaries, and link purpose statements.
  • Tooling: Axe DevTools, Siteimprove, and manual keyboard testing baked into QA checklist.
  • Documentation: Each deliverable includes an accessibility conformance note, logging any exceptions for Sport Wales’ accessibility statement.
